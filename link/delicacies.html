 Famous Waray Delicacies

BINAGOL


The Waraynon's pride, Binagol, is a famous delicacy produced by the municipality of Dagami, Leyte. It is from a shredded "Talyan", a root crop similar to Gabi mixed with butter, egg, coconut milk, nuts, and sugar. This mixture is cooked and put inside a polished coconut shell, wrapped uniquely with banana leaves and nylon after cooking, making it presentable. "Binagol" came from a root word "bagol" which means coconut shell.

Ingredients:
4 medium, polished coconut shells
4 egg yolks
3/4 cups shredded Taro root crop
3/4 cup brown sugar
1 cup creamy coconut milk
1/2 can full cream condensed milk
Wilted banana leaves
Nylon string for tying

Procedure:
1. On a pan, combine the shredded Taro, sugar, and coconut milk.
2. Cook over medium heat for about 6 minutes while continuously stirring.
3. Reduce heat and cook for 10 minutes.
4. Add the condensed milk.
5. Cook for another 20 minutes and stir it continuously.
6. Fill the polished coconut shell with mixture and make a well in the center.
7. Fill the well with raw egg yolks and cover the top with mixture, spreading it smoothly.
8. Cover the filled coconut shells with two layers banana leaves and tie it securely with nylon.
9. Place in a steamer and cook for another 30 minutes.

Moron, especially in Leyte and Samar, is one of the delicacies of the Eastern Visayas. It is a kind of "suman" made from glutinous rice also known as "malagkit".

Ingredients:
• 1/2 cup glutinous rice or “malagkit”
• 3/4 cup coconut milk
• 1-1/2 cup ordinary rice
• 1-1/2 cup sugar
• Tablia (chocolate bar) or cocoa
• Melted margarine or butter

Procedures:
1. Soak the ‘’malagkit’’ (glutinous rice) and ordinary rice overnight. Grind the following day.
2. Soak both ground rice in coconut milk until soft.
3. Add sugar and tablia (chocolate bar) or cocoa.
4. Cook over low fire, constantly stirring until thick. Set aside and cool.
5. Prepare the leaves for wrapping by heating by over low fire.
6. Brush the leaves with butter.
7. Put 2 tbsp. of the mixture in every wrap. Tie with string. Repeat until all are wrapped.
8. Cook/steam for half an hour in a steamer or double boiler container.
